Digital Cost Structure Framework

Classifying digital expenditures to align technology investment with business strategy, inspired by the "Fit for Growth" methodology.

The Core of the Fit for Growth Framework

Company's Strategy: Articulate the capabilities that are essential to win in the market.
1. Focus on Differentiating Capabilities

Invest resources in the distinct capabilities that allow the organization to outperform competitors.

→
2. Align Cost Structure

Align the entire cost structure with the strategy by cutting non-value-adding expenses (Bad Costs) to fund investments in critical areas (Good Costs).

→
3. Reorganize for Growth

Redesign the organization, processes, and systems to unlock potential and create agility for growth.

Applying FFG to the IT Organization

1. Shift the IT Perspective

Transition IT to a "Strategic Partner" that Leveraging digital technology to help businesses achieve their strategic goals and gain a competitive advantage.

2. Design with a Zero-Based Mindset

We will ignore the current org chart—its boxes, lines, and legacy structures. We are building for the future, not servicing the past. Example Digitalization is often not about business owners moving traditional processes online (digitizing old processes), but rather about "reimagining the alternative process."

3. Reorganize for growth

" Move away from siloed IT model to cross-functional teams that own a business capability end-to-end (B2B, B2C, SC visibility team). These teams are comprised of business experts, Application architect, technology architect and data analysts who are funded and empowered to deliver business outcomes, not just complete tasks.

Enable Culture Evolution: Reconfiguring our IT engine to be leaner, faster, and laser focused on what truly matters to our business

IT Cost Management Matrix

Strategic Rationale

"Resources to be liberated and reallocated."

This is spending that is redundant, non-value-adding, or misaligned with current strategy. It is a drag on growth and must be aggressively removed.

Digital Examples
  • "Zombie Projects" unusued, no longer aligned to strategy
  • Unused or overlapping Software Licenses
  • Uncontrolled "IT" spending
  • Uncompile security standard application
Improvement Goal & Actions
GOAL: Eliminate and Simplify
  • Application Rationalization: Actively decommission old systems.
  • Toll gate: Control and consolidate purchasing.
  • Kill Projects: Create a culture where it's safe to stop failing initiatives.

Strategic Rationale

"Required to keep the business running."

These are the most basic operational costs. Non-discretionary and non-differentiating, but failure here causes severe disruption.

Digital Examples
  • Server & Data Center Maintenance
  • IT Helpdesk & Basic Support
  • CMS
  • Email, VPN, e-Hr
  • O365 office, Google work space
Improvement Goal & Actions
GOAL: Maximize Efficiency, Minimize Cost
  • Standardize & Automate: Use self-service portals.
  • Cloud Migration: Shift from CAPEX to OPEX (e.g., Microsoft 365).
  • Outsource: Use external vendors for non-core functions.

Strategic Rationale

"Required to stay in the game."

These are industry-standard capabilities. Not having them puts you at a disadvantage, but having them doesn't create a unique advantage.

Digital Examples
  • Standard ERP System (SAP, F1, NetSuite)
  • Bluenet, E-Ordering, E-Commerce, PromptPlus,
  • Regulatory & Compliance
  • Cybersecurity
  • Modern Data Platform
Improvement Goal & Actions
GOAL: Achieve Standard at Optimal Cost
  • Risk-base investment: align security investments directly to the most significant business risks, ensuring every Baht is spent on meaningful protection, not just compliance checklists.
  • Global best practise standard framework
  • FinOps: Eliminate waste, continuous optimization,Improving Forecasting and Budgeting

Strategic Rationale

"Required to create a competitive advantage."

These investments are directly linked to your firm's differentiating capabilities. This is where you should over-invest relative to competitors.

Digital Examples
  • New Bunisess Model (F/C, Tiny customer, e-monney)
  • Agile/DevOps Teams for Rapid Product Innovation
  • RPA -> IPA
  • 6 pathways to North Star
Improvement Goal & Actions
GOAL: Invest Strategically, Accelerate ROI
  • Protect the Budget: Shield these funds from broad cost-cutting.
  • Measure Everything: Focus intensely on business value and ROI.
  • Attract Top Talent: Invest in the best people to run these initiatives.

The Executive Mandate

The primary task for leadership is to systematically categorize all digital spending into these four buckets. The goal is to aggressively eliminate Bad Costs, optimize Lights On and Table Stakes, and strategically reallocate the liberated resources to fuel the Good Costs that directly drive competitive advantage and growth.
