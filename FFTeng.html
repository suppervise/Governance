Fit for Growth (FFG) Framework

A Guide to Building an IT Organization that Drives Sustainable Growth

The Core of the Fit for Growth Framework

Company's Strategy: Articulate the capabilities that are essential to win in the market.
1. Focus on Differentiating Capabilities

Invest resources in the distinct capabilities that allow the organization to outperform competitors.

→
2. Align Cost Structure

Align the entire cost structure with the strategy by cutting non-value-adding expenses (Bad Costs) to fund investments in critical areas (Good Costs).

→
3. Reorganize for Growth

Redesign the organization, processes, and systems to unlock potential and create agility for growth.

Enable Culture Evolution: Foster a culture that embraces change and embeds the FFG mindset into the organization's DNA.

Applying FFG to the IT Organization

1. Shift the IT Perspective

Transition IT from a "Cost Center" to a "Strategic Partner" that leverages technology to build differentiating business capabilities.

2. Design with a Zero-Based Mindset

Ask, "If we were to build an IT organization today to win, what would it look like?" instead of making incremental changes to the existing structure.

3. Invest Strategically

Clearly classify IT expenditures to "eliminate" non-essential costs and "reinvest" resources into technologies that provide a true competitive advantage.

IT Cost Management Matrix

Cost Category Strategic Description IT Cost Examples Management Plan
Bad Costs Redundant, non-value-adding, or strategically misaligned expenses. This is the "fat" to be eliminated.
  • Maintenance of redundant Legacy Systems
  • Unused software licenses or redundant applications
  • "Shadow IT" - Uncontrolled cloud services
  • Misaligned "Zombie Projects"
 Goal: Eliminate & Simplify
  • Plan to decommission unnecessary systems
  • Centralize IT procurement and governance
  • Be bold in killing projects that no longer deliver value
Lights On Essential expenses to keep the business running. Non-negotiable but not a differentiator. Failure is catastrophic.
  • Data Center & Server maintenance
  • IT Helpdesk & User Support
  • Core Network Infrastructure
  • Corporate email system
 Goal: Optimize & Automate
  • Accelerate Cloud Migration
  • Maximize automation for routine tasks
  • Consider outsourcing for non-core functions
Table Stakes Required expenses to compete in the market. The industry standard. Lacking them is a significant disadvantage.
  • ERP Systems (e.g., SAP, Oracle)
  • Basic CRM system for sales teams
  • Fundamental Cybersecurity (Firewall, Antivirus)
  • Standard corporate website
 Goal: Standardize & Control Cost
  • Avoid unnecessary customization ("gold plating")
  • Leverage standard SaaS solutions
  • Optimize license management and vendor contracts
Good Costs Strategic investments directly linked to "Differentiating Capabilities." This budget must be protected and increased to fuel growth.
  • Advanced Data Analytics platform for customer insights
  • Agile/DevOps teams to accelerate digital product delivery
  • AI/ML for Supply Chain optimization
  • Advanced Digital Marketing & Automation tools
 Goal: Invest & Accelerate
  • Protect this budget from across-the-board cuts
  • Rigorously measure Return on Investment (ROI)
  • Attract and retain top talent in these key areas
