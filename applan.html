The Governance Engine

A clear, practical framework to ensure all digital initiatives are secure, compliant, and deliver business value.

The Purpose of Governance

Governance exists to answer three critical business questions, ensuring IT is a strategic enabler, not a cost center. This aligns with frameworks like COBIT.

  • Value Delivery: Ensure every digital investment provides measurable ROI and supports the Group's objectives.
  • Risk Management: Systematically identify, assess, and mitigate risks, from cybersecurity threats to project failures, in line with ISO 27001 and NIST CSF.
  • Compliance: Adhere to all legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations across our diverse industries and regions.

What We Govern: The Unified Framework

We govern through a unified set of standards, policies, and architectural blueprints. These are mandatory and form the "rulebook" for all digital activities.

  • Digital Policies: Group-wide rules for security, data privacy, and acceptable use.
  • Architectural Blueprints: Mandatory standards for Application, Data, and Technology architecture.
  • Standard Practices: The approved processes for managing services (ITIL), projects, and changes.
  • Checklist Templates: Practical, tiered checklists derived from our standards to assess new and existing assets.

How We Implement: Our 6-Step Process

1. Create Mandatory Standards

The Governance Team defines the "rulebook," which architecture teams transform into practical checklists and procedures.

2. Create Visibility

We partner with local IT to map all digital assets (apps, data, etc.) in our central Enterprise Architecture tool.

3. Analyze Gaps

We compare existing assets against our standards to identify risks, technical debt, and compliance gaps.

4. Plan & Remediate

We create and fund prioritized plans to close identified gaps, managed as formal projects.

5. Embed in Workflows

We "shift left" by embedding our standards into project lifecycles, procurement, and CI/CD pipelines to prevent future gaps.

6. Monitor & Automate

We use automated tools to continuously monitor compliance, collect audit evidence, and detect anomalies using AIOps.

When Governance Applies: The Lifecycle Tollgates

Governance is not a single event; it's embedded at key stages of any new initiative to ensure quality from the start.

  1. Tollgate 1: Project Initiation
    A "light" checklist must be completed to assess initial risk, data sensitivity, and architectural impact before project approval.
  2. Tollgate 2: Design Approval
    A detailed design must be approved by the Architecture Review Board (ARB) against the full standard checklist before development starts.
  3. Tollgate 3: Go-Live Readiness
    A final "Go-Live" checklist, including security testing and operational readiness, must be approved by the Change Advisory Board (CAB).
