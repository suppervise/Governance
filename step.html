Operationalizing Governance: Our 6-Step Engine

This is our practical, repeatable process for embedding strong governance into everything we do. It's how we move from theory to daily practice.




1. Create Mandatory Standards - "The Rulebook"

We define a single, authoritative set of rules, policies, and blueprints that govern all digital activities.

Key Activities:
  • Develop tiered Reference Architectures (our "paved roads").
  • Form an Architecture Review Board (ARB) with cross-functional members.
  • Transform standards into practical "Living Standard" checklist templates.

2. Create Visibility - "The Map"

We partner with local IT to discover and map every digital asset (apps, data, infrastructure) into a central repository.

Key Activities:
  • Use our Enterprise Architecture (EA) Tool as the single source of truth.
  • Focus on mapping connections and dependencies, not just listing assets.
  • Position this as a partnership to help local teams manage their own environments better.

3. Analyze Gaps - "The Diagnosis"

We systematically compare our asset inventory ("The Map") against our standards ("The Rulebook") to identify all deviations.

Key Activities:
  • Automate analysis using the EA tool to create heatmaps and dashboards.
  • Identify technical debt, security risks, and compliance violations.
  • Prioritize all gaps using a formal, risk-based approach (Business Impact x Likelihood).

4. Plan & Close Gaps - "The Treatment Plan"

We transform the prioritized list of gaps into funded, actionable projects with clear ownership and deadlines.

Key Activities:
  • Integrate remediation plans into the formal IT project portfolio.
  • Assign clear ownership for systemic gaps (central team) and specific gaps (local teams).
  • Track all remediation efforts transparently in a central tool (e.g., Jira, Azure DevOps).

5. Embed in Workflows - "The Prevention"

We "shift left," making compliance the easiest path by building our standards directly into day-to-day processes.

Key Activities:
  • Establish formal "Tollgates" in the project lifecycle (Initiation, Design, Go-Live).
  • Integrate automated security and compliance checks into CI/CD pipelines.
  • Update ITSM (ITIL) processes like Change Management and Procurement to enforce standards.

6. Monitor & Automate - "The Immune System"

We create a living, breathing system that continuously monitors our environment and automatically detects or prevents deviations.

Key Activities:
  • Deploy real-time monitoring dashboards for compliance and security posture.
  • Automate evidence collection for audits, reducing manual effort.
  • Leverage AIOps to automatically detect configuration drift and anomalous activity.

From a Linear Process to a Continuous Flywheel

This isn't a one-time project. Step 6 feeds directly back into Step 2 and 3, creating a virtuous cycle of continuous improvement. This is how we build a mature, resilient, and data-driven governance capability.

Continuous Improvement

We are always assessing and refining our standards and controls.

Data-Driven

Our decisions are based on real-time data from our systems, not guesswork.

Increasingly Automated
