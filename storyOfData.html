From a Chaotic Kitchen to a Smart Food Court

A Strategy to Transform "Spaghetti" Data into Valuable Products

What Does Our "Data Kitchen" Look Like Today?

we're facing what's known as "spaghetti pipelines."

Everyone Builds Their Own Kitchen

Each team builds its own data silo pipeline to cook 'fast food' dishes. The result is...

  • Redundant: Dozens of small kitchens making similar meals.
  • Inconsistent: The recipe for "customer" is different in every kitchen.
  • Wasteful: The same ingredients (data) are bought repeatedly and spoil.

Or, We Have a Single "Central Kitchen"

All teams send their 'orders' to a single, central data teamâ€”the traditional approach. The result is...

  • Standardized: Every dish uses the same recipe.
  • Bottlenecked: A long waiting line to get your food.
  • Doesn't Meet Needs: The chef doesn't understand the customer's taste.

The Solution: Build the Ultimate Food Court

We will blend the "discipline" of an Enterprise Architect with the "creativity" of chefs (Data Analytic Teams).

1. The food Court Manager (Data preperation)

We don't cook the food; we build the best "venue" and "ingredients."

  • Build the Infrastructure: Provide top-tier utilities like water, power, and gas (the Data Platform).
  • Set Health & Safety Codes: Define standards for cleanliness and quality (Data Governance & Quality).

2. The Restaurant Owners (The Role of Data analytic/analyst Teams)

Each domain team becomes a "chef," specializing in their own cuisine.

  • Create Signature Dishes: Build high-quality "Data Products," like a "360 Customer View" dish.
  • Responsible for Quality: Ensure their ingredients are fresh and the taste is consistent.

The Results of Building This "Food Court"

Less Time to Market for New Use Cases

Like opening a new restaurant in a fully equipped food court instead of building from scratch.

Lower Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)

No more redundant kitchens, and ingredients (data) are shared efficiently.

Enables New Business Opportunities

With a menu of quality data products, we can create new services we never could before.

It's time to transform our "chaotic kitchen" into a "smart food court" that drives sustainable growth.
