Governance & Compliance Team

Ensuring IT aligns with business objectives, ethical standards, and regulatory requirements while enabling innovation.

Team Overview & Rationale

Mission: Establish a comprehensive framework for directing and controlling IT resources to ensure they are aligned with business goals, secure, compliant, and deliver optimal value. scope of "Governance and Security" beyond just policies and controls! It should encompass a more holistic view of IT management to ensure alignment and value creation.

Why This Team is Essential: Without this team, CGS faces significant risks including data breaches, non-compliance penalties, reputational damage, misaligned IT investments, and inefficient processes. This team is the conscience and a key enabler of controlled, sustainable digital transformation.

(This team's role as "housekeeper" and strategic enabler becomes even more critical with the consolidated Technology Architecture team. They ensure the "what" and "how" from other teams align with the "why" and "rules" of the business.)

Core Team Structure & Key Roles:
  • To establish, maintain, and enforce a comprehensive IT governance, risk, and compliance
  • To adopt process for ensuring all IT activities align with CGS's business objectives and madatory standard
  • To Comunicate and collaborate with IT representative of CGS's sub business
  • To take care of agreement and vendor management
  • To develope IT strategic of workforce development and cost optimazation
  • To motitor and benchmark with global practice's IT management interm of budgeting, technology adoption, workforce's skill set
Required Skills:

Governance frameworks (COBIT, ISO 27001, NIST), risk assessment, relevant laws (PDPA), IT audit, policy development, vendor management, communication, change management.

36-Month Action Plan & Roadmap

Overall Goal / End-State Vision: A mature and agile GRC framework is deeply embedded across CGS, fostering a culture of compliance and risk awareness. IT investments are strategically aligned, risks are proactively managed, and CGS consistently meets or exceeds regulatory and ethical standards, enabling trustworthy innovation.

Key Initiatives & Activities: Review/update core IT policies, Establish/reaffirm IT Governance Committee, Implement Vendor Risk Management program, Roll out security awareness/training, Develop/implement IT Risk Management Framework, Formalize EA coordination.

Key Milestones:

Q2 (M6)

Core IT policies updated. IT Gov Committee operational.

Q4 (M12)

IT Risk Management Framework implemented.

Key Initiatives: Implement/enhance Governance tools, Conduct internal IT audits, Optimize IT budgeting/financial governance, Extend risk assessments to emerging tech, Develop IT team skills & IT champions.

Key Milestones: Governance tool (Phase 1) implemented (M18). First cycle of internal IT audits completed (M18). Measurable improvement in governance maturity (M24).

Key Initiatives: Pursue external audits/certifications, Continuously measure/improve Governance framework, Embed GRC as a strategic enabler for innovation, Foster ethical/compliant tech use culture, Automate compliance checks by AI.

Key Milestones: Successful Automatic audit or Abnormally notification (M30). Embed all Policy and guideline into Business process(inter lock) (M36).

Measurable Outcomes & KPIs:
  • Reduction in IT-related audit findings by 50%.
  • No incident issues related with pooring governance. 0%
  • Percentage of IT projects adhering to governance gates. by 80%
  • Percent of Vendor achive Mandatory standard trainning by 100%.
  • Percentage of employees pass e-policy and security awareness test by 85%.
  • IT's spending compare with peer in industrial.

Business Impact

  • Reduced Risk: Proactive identification and mitigation of IT-related risks.
  • Enhanced Trust: Increased confidence from customers, partners, and regulators.
  • Optimized Investments: Ensures IT spending aligns with business strategy.
  • Improved Operational Stability: Standardized processes and controls.
  • Legal & Regulatory Adherence: Avoids fines and reputational damage.
  • Ethical & Responsible Innovation: Guides adoption of new technologies.
