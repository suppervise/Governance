IT Action Plan

A) Business-Led Key Initiatives

This group of initiatives is focused on directly supporting and responding to the needs of business units to enhance competitive capabilities and drive growth in alignment with corporate objectives.

1. Existing Business Capability Enhancement

Initiatives started prior to the North Star program to address ongoing business needs and enhance operational capabilities. These must continue to ensure business continuity.

  • Enhance existing ERP/CRM systems to support more complex operations.
  • Maintain and support legacy applications critical to business operations.
  • Support specialized projects for individual business units as previously planned.

Goal: To maintain business continuity and maximize the efficiency of daily operations.

2. North Star Transformation Support Plan

A plan specifically designed to successfully support the organizational transformation under the North Star program.

Phase 1: Design & Preparation (Present - End of 2025)
  • Co-design 'To-Be' Processes: Work closely with business teams to provide technical consultation on technology capabilities and constraints.
  • Analyze & Design IT Architecture: Conduct a Gap Analysis of current systems and design the Target Architecture to support new processes.
  • Plan System Changes: Create a roadmap for upgrading, developing, or procuring necessary systems, with a primary focus on Order Fulfillment and Data Integration.

Goal: To deliver a complete and approved Process & IT blueprint, ready for execution in 2026.

Phase 2: Execution & Transformation (January 2026 onwards)
  • Implementation: Launch the new IT organizational structure and begin system development projects according to the approved plan.
  • Change Management: Communicate and train users to ensure a smooth adoption of new processes and tools.
  • Performance Monitoring: Track results against defined KPIs and gather feedback for continuous improvement.

Goal: To successfully implement new systems and processes, measure business impact, and drive continuous improvement.

B) IT-Led Key Initiatives

Initiatives driven by the IT department to build a strong, efficient, and secure technology foundation that supports long-term business growth.

1. Best Practices for Governance & Security

To ensure all digital platforms are highly available, secure, and perform optimally.

  • Establish Security Policies: Develop and enforce a comprehensive cybersecurity policy across all systems to protect corporate data.
  • Form an Architecture Review Board (ARB): To ensure all new IT projects align with the enterprise architecture, avoiding redundancy and technical debt.
  • Plan for Incident Response & Disaster Recovery: Prepare and test plans to ensure rapid recovery of critical systems in case of disruption.

Goal: To build business confidence by ensuring the highest levels of IT stability and security.

2. Optimization of Resources

Following the consolidation of IT units, there is a significant opportunity to reduce cost redundancies and improve service delivery efficiency to business units.

  • Application Portfolio Management: Survey and assess all applications to decide whether to retire, retain, or revamp them, aiming to reduce redundant license and maintenance costs.
  • Infrastructure Optimization: Consolidate scattered servers, storage systems, and networks to reduce energy and maintenance expenses.
  • Cloud FinOps: Analyze and optimize cloud service usage to ensure the company pays only for necessary resources and achieves maximum cost-effectiveness.

Goal: To reduce redundant IT costs and enhance service delivery to better support the business.
