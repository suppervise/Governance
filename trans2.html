12-Week Strategic Work Plan: Customer-Centric Business & IT Transformation

Overall Project Division

The work is divided into two main periods:

  • Period 1: Mid-September to December – Business designs customer channels, processes including 02c, supply chain, P2P, business rules, and authority metrics with IT support and advice. IT consolidates platforms into an Application Portfolio to reduce redundancy and identify cost-saving opportunities. A software developer will be engaged to assess resources, budget, and staffing needs to support new business models and architecture. The result will be detailed designs and application enhancements focusing on order fulfillment and data integration for management reports.
  • Period 2: Starting January onwards – Team operates under the new organizational structure, implements app changes and workflows, revamps applications as designed, and continuously monitors progress and results.

12-Week Strategic Design & Foundation Phase

Phase 1: Foundation & Discovery (Weeks 1-2)

Objective: Establish a detailed baseline of current "As-Is" environment and capture high-level business requirements for the future "To-Be" model.

Major Activities:

  • Stakeholder Kick-off & Alignment: Workshops with initiative champions and TMO to validate objectives, scope, and key interdependencies across 6 pathways.
  • As-Is Application Portfolio Analysis: Rapid inventory of key enterprise systems (ERP, CRM, SCM, Bluenet, etc.) to identify optimization, consolidation, and cost reduction (Cloud FinOps quick wins).
  • Business Process Discovery: Engage Go-To-Market and Modern Retail working groups to map current key processes like Order-to-Cash (O2C), Procure-to-Pay (P2P), and customer support.
  • Conceptual Business Vision: Document core principles of the customer-centric strategy, translating them into architectural principles and business capability requirements.

Key Deliverables:

  • As-Is Application Landscape & Optimization Report detailing system redundancies and cost-saving opportunities.
  • High-Level 'As-Is' Process Maps outlining current cross-functional processes.
  • Statement of Architecture Work defining scope, objectives, and constraints aligned with stakeholders.

TOGAF Alignment: Preliminary Phase & Phase A: Architecture Vision.

Phase 2: Target Architecture & Organizational Design (Weeks 3-8)

Objective: Define future-state architecture across Business, Application, Data, and Technology domains and design supporting Process & IT organizational structure.

Major Activities:

  • To-Be Business Process Design: Intensive workshops with B2C, B2B, and joint groups to create integrated customer-centric processes with seamless end-to-end customer journey.
  • Target Application & Data Architecture Design:
    • Map new business processes to app and data requirements.
    • Define future-state application architecture including central CRM, upgraded ERP, and customer data platform.
    • Design data architecture focusing on "single source of truth" and data governance.
  • Target Technology Architecture & Infrastructure: Propose foundational tech platform and a clear cloud adoption strategy (IaaS/PaaS/SaaS).
  • IT Organizational Structure & Staffing Model Design:
    • Define core Process & IT organization functions (Business Relationship Management, Enterprise Architecture, Data & Analytics, Infrastructure & Operations).
    • Create draft organizational chart with key roles and required skills.
    • Design IT governance framework including Architecture Review Board.

Key Deliverables:

  • High-Level Target Architecture Blueprints (Business, Application, Data, Technology).
  • Architecture Gap Analysis (As-Is vs. To-Be).
  • Draft IT Organizational Chart & Role Descriptions.
  • Proposed IT Governance Model.

TOGAF Alignment: Phase B, C, and D (Business, Information Systems, Technology Architectures).

Phase 3: Implementation Roadmap & Handoff Preparation (Weeks 9-12)

Objective: Translate architectural design into an actionable implementation plan and prepare for handoff to implementation partner.

Major Activities:

  • Develop Implementation Roadmap for MVP: Define sequenced projects over 1-2 years prioritizing critical capabilities in order fulfillment and data integration.
  • Define Workflow System Requirements: Detailed requirements for workflow systems supporting new business processes.
  • Develop Business Case & Financial Model: Refined cost estimates, benefits including EBITDA impact, and value realization timeline.
  • Prepare Implementation Partner Briefing Package consolidating architecture, gap analysis, organizational model, and MVP roadmap.
  • Final Review & Approval: Present complete design package to Steering Committee for formal approval.

Key Deliverables:

  • Sequenced Implementation Roadmap (1-2 year horizon).
  • MVP Definition & Scope Document.
  • Refined Business Case and Financials.
  • Comprehensive Briefing Package for Implementation Partner.

TOGAF Alignment: Phase E & F (Opportunities & Solutions, Migration Planning).

Summary of Periods & Focus Areas

  • First Period (Mid-September to Year-End): Business designs customer channels including 02c, supply chain, P2P, business rules, and authority metrics. IT participates in design, provides advice, works on application portfolio consolidation, identifies cost savings, and prepares for software developer engagement and new organizational design.
  • Second Period (New Year onwards): Teams work under new organizational structure executing application rollouts, managing workflows, performing revamps, and monitoring outcomes continuously.

This phased, detailed plan ensures strong alignment between business and IT, a clear roadmap with quick wins for order fulfillment and data integration, and preparation for long-term success through measured execution and governance.
